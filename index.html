Infrastructure, Risk, and Resilience.

I am a civil engineer with experience working on infrastructure projects across the Caribbean and the United States, including highways, coastal infrastructure, and housing developments. Through this work, I saw how infrastructure failures can disrupt entire communities and economies.

I grew up in Jamaica and began my engineering career working on infrastructure projects across the Caribbean — a region that faces hurricanes, flooding, and coastal hazards every year. Working in these environments, I saw firsthand how vulnerable infrastructure can disrupt entire communities — cutting off access to hospitals, jobs, schools, and essential services.

Those experiences shaped the direction of my career. Today, my work focuses on infrastructure resilience, hazard risk, and decision-support tools that help communities and decision-makers understand what infrastructure is most critical, what is most at risk, and what actions will have the greatest impact before and after disasters.

Having worked in hurricane-prone regions and now living and working in the United States, I am interested in infrastructure resilience at scale — from small communities to national infrastructure systems — and how data, engineering, and technology can help us build and protect more resilient communities.
